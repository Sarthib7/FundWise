Round 3 · Researched & refined
Marks worth wearing

Four abstract, professional concepts pulled from how the best fintech and crypto-native brands actually work. Each is a single confident shape — not literal symbolism. The gradient does the warmth; the form does the credibility.

What the market taught us
The principles guiding round three
01 · Subtract
The best fintech marks reduce meaning rather than add it. Stripe avoids symbolism entirely. PayPal subtracted, didn’t reinvent. We stop trying to draw a "split."
02 · One bold gesture
Phantom = a ghost. MetaMask = a fox. Chainlink = a link. One confident shape beats a clever composite of three meanings every time.
03 · Modular system
PayPal’s monogram works alone in app icons; the wordmark works for headers. We design the mark and the wordmark to live separately or together.
04 · Trust × tech
Fintech logos must balance stability and innovation. Sharp geometry reads as tech; depth and weight read as trust. We use both.
05 · Sketchable
Paul Rand’s test: distinctive, memorable, clear. If you can’t sketch it from memory, it’s not working. All four marks here pass.
06 · Owns the gradient
FundWise green is your most distinctive asset. We let it carry weight — not as decoration, but as the mark itself.

CONCEPT 12 ─ STRATA Layers of liquidity
Strata

Three soft horizontal bands stacked at slight angles, fading from deep forest into bright lime. Reads as layered liquidity — settlements stacking, ledgers compounding. Sophisticated, abstract, distinctly modern. The mark IS the gradient: no white shapes fighting for attention, just three slabs of color.

Why this works
Owns the gradientThe form IS color. No competing white shapes.
Reads as motionSlight rotation gives it energy without being playful.
Crypto-nativeEchoes Solana’s parallel bars but warmer, less harsh.
Scales cleanThree slabs hold their identity from 16px to 1024px.

CONCEPT 13 ─ APERTURE A monogram with a window
Aperture

A solid rounded square in the gradient, with a precise geometric F-cut carved through it — negative-space monogram. The F is the hinge of "FundWise"; the carved-out form lets the gradient breathe through it. This is the most "Stripe-tier" of the four: confident, restrained, immediately ownable.

Why this works
Negative spaceThe gradient flows through the cut — same energy as Apple, NBC, FedEx.
Monogram, modernAn F that doesn’t feel corporate-stuffy.
Highly recognizableDistinctive silhouette at thumbnail scale.
Universal lockupsWorks white-on-grad, grad-on-white, mono.

CONCEPT 14 ─ COIN A token, abstracted
Coin

A perfect circle, two-toned along a soft diagonal — like a coin caught mid-rotation. Half rich green, half luminous lime, with a hairline highlight where the two meet. Reads as currency, unity, and motion. The simplest possible mark; the strongest token-economy resonance.

Why this works
Perfect circleThe most enduring shape in finance — coin, token, seal.
Maximum simplicityNo literal symbols. Pure form, pure color.
Two-tone identityThe diagonal split is uniquely yours — sketchable in seconds.
Crypto-nativeLives comfortably next to SOL, USDC, ETH.

CONCEPT 15 ─ MERIDIAN A signature in motion
Meridian

A single continuous stroke — bold, calligraphic, drawn in one breath. The shape suggests an F flowing into a curve, a financial signature, a current of money. Custom gradient stroke (no flat fill) gives it dimensionality. Most distinctive of the set; the only one that could not exist as someone else’s mark.

Why this works
Most distinctiveCustom shape — no template, no clip-art lineage.
Reads as FWithout spelling it out. Subtle monogram.
Calligraphic energyConfident, drawn-in-one-stroke. Personal.
Stroke gradientThe line itself transitions — a move very few crypto brands make.
